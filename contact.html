Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about website optimization and our services.

How long does it typically take to see improvements after optimizing website speed?

Most website speed optimizations show immediate improvements in loading times. However, search engine recognition of these improvements can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, depending on how frequently Google crawls your site. Core Web Vitals data in Google Search Console typically updates within 28 days.

What's the difference between technical SEO and on-page SEO?

Technical SEO focuses on improving website infrastructure to help search engines crawl and index your site more effectively (site speed, mobile-friendliness, site architecture). On-page SEO involves optimizing individual page content and HTML elements (title tags, headers, content quality, internal linking). Both are essential for ranking well in search results.

How many backlinks do I need to start seeing ranking improvements?

Quality matters more than quantity when it comes to backlinks. Even a few high-quality backlinks from authoritative websites in your niche can significantly impact your rankings. Focus on building relationships and creating link-worthy content rather than pursuing a specific number of backlinks.

What are the most important metrics to track for website performance?

The key metrics to track include:

  • Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) - should be under 2.5 seconds
  • First Input Delay (FID) - should be under 100 milliseconds
  • Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) - should be under 0.1
  • Time to First Byte (TTFB) - should be under 800ms
  • Overall page load time - ideally under 3 seconds
